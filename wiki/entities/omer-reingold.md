## About

Professor of Computer Science at Stanford. Gödel Prize laureate (2009) for undirected ST-connectivity in log-space; co-creator of zig-zag graph products and expander constructions; Knuth Prize (2024). Works on complexity theory, cryptography, pseudorandomness, and more recently AI alignment foundations.

## Contributions / Works

- **[On the Impossibility of Separating Intelligence from Judgment (Ball-Gluch-Goldwasser-Kreuter-Reingold-Rothblum 2025)](../sources/filter-impossibility-ai-alignment.md)** — the Goldwasser-style filter-impossibility theorem for LLM alignment; the long-awaited Pillar III anchor.
- Foundational work on pseudorandomness, derandomization, zig-zag graph products, and cryptographic hardness.
- Recent focus on fairness, alignment, and the complexity-theoretic foundations of AI safety.
